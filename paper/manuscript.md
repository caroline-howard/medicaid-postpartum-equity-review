# Medicaid Postpartum Coverage & Maternal Health Equity Systematic Review

## Abstract

Draft placeholder for a systematic review of how Medicaid postpartum coverage extensions relate to maternal health access, continuity of care, and health equity for low-income and medically vulnerable postpartum populations in the United States.

## Introduction

Medicaid finances a substantial share of births in the United States, and postpartum coverage stability may influence access to care, continuity of services, behavioral health treatment, and equity in maternal health outcomes. This review focuses on evidence and policy sources related to postpartum Medicaid/CHIP coverage extensions, especially 12-month postpartum coverage.

## Methods

This project uses a reproducible PRISMA 2020-style workflow. Searches are conducted in PubMed and OpenAlex, with optional Crossref metadata enrichment. Candidate records are deduplicated and prepared for human title/abstract screening and full-text eligibility review. Automation supports retrieval, metadata cleaning, deduplication, relevance scoring, and count tracking; it does not make final inclusion decisions.
